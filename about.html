About

I think of my work as a dance—an ever-longing play of life, where self dissolves and perception peels back to childhood wonder. My pieces aren’t passive objects but living invitations: you, the observer and participant, become part of the conceptual loop, embodied within the art.

While labels feel constricting, I gravitate toward contemporary neo-expressionism, drawing inspiration from quantum mechanics, philosophy, consciousness studies, extraterrestrial imaginaries, meditation, psychotropic exploration, and love.

Artistic Statement

My practice sits at the intersection of internal cognition and external representation—art as both process and artifact. I frame each piece as a feedback loop, where memory, ritual, and neural resonance converge to create an immersive experience.
